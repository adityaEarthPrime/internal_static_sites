Compensation

One global rate per role

All salary ranges are global and reflect the level of talent — not the location of the hire. A Deployment Lead in Bangkok and one in Bengaluru do the same work and create the same value. They are paid the same. The range within each role reflects seniority only.

The principle: We are compensating for value created and level of operation — not for cost of living. If someone in a lower-cost city asks why they're paid the same as someone in an expensive city, the answer is: because you are equally valuable. Equity fills the gap for early hires who take on more risk.

What the range is for
Where someone sits on the junior-to-senior spectrum within their role title. A DL with 4 years starts at $95K. A DL with 7 years of UNDP and ADB experience starts at $115K. A seniority conversation, transparent with every candidate.
What the range is not for
Location. Cost of living. "Market rate in their city." The same role in Bengaluru and Bangkok carries the same number — because the value created is the same and we don't want to build a two-tier culture.
How equity fills the gap
Early hires take more risk. Their equity bands reflect this honestly — higher percentages for Phase 1 hires than Phase 3 hires at the same level. Equity is not a token; it should feel meaningful.
When to revisit
After each funding round and annually. Salaries should grow with the company's ability to pay. Exceptional people leave when their comp stops reflecting their contribution — not when they find a higher number elsewhere.
Deployment model

One track, not two

There is one Deployment Lead profile, one career path, and one standard of excellence. The platform does the heavy lifting — the DL's job evolves alongside it.

The evolution of a Deployment Lead: Early in a contract, they are domain-heavy and hands-on — producing the analysis the Judgement Engine will eventually automate. Over time, as the platform matures, they shift toward configuring, operating, and expanding it within the client. Same person, same relationship, growing leverage. The platform makes them more powerful with each engagement — it never makes them redundant.
Career arc: Deployment Lead (4–6 yrs exp) → Senior Deployment Lead (7–10 yrs) → Head of Deployment (10+ yrs). No second-class tier, no consulting-vs-product split. The people who join early and grow with the platform become Verdera's most durable asset — they carry both the institutional trust and the muscle memory of how the platform was built.
The FDE parallel: This is how Palantir's Forward Deployed Engineers worked — one role that started close to the data and ended up running critical infrastructure. The platform made them more powerful, not obsolete. Verdera Deployment Leads follow the same arc. The analogy is worth using in recruiting conversations.
Global team

Global from Day 1,
not global eventually

Verdera is not an Indian company going global — it is a global company headquartered in Bengaluru. That distinction needs to show up in job descriptions, the website, comp philosophy, and who gets hired as Deployment Leads from Day 1. The Bengaluru core is dense and fast; the global layer follows contract geography, not speculation.

Hub-and-deploy: Bengaluru is the product and operations brain. New York (Aditya's frequent base) anchors the investor and development finance narrative layer. Bangkok is the Southeast Asia client base. New geographies open only when a specific contract or pipeline signal warrants a permanent hire — not on the basis of market potential alone.
Hiring
Global talent pool, Bengaluru density
Deployment Leads and domain experts are hired from wherever the best climate expertise lives — not just India. UNDP alumni in Nairobi, adaptation specialists in London, ex-ADB people in Manila are fair game from Day 1. The platform team concentrates in Bengaluru for execution speed.
Rhythms
Async-first, sprint-together
Default to async documentation and written communication. Quarterly in-person team sprints in Bengaluru — all remote hires fly in. These are culture, alignment, and product immersion events. Not status update meetings.
Language
English as the operating language
All internal docs, proposals, and platforms in English from Day 1. Not a concession — a deliberate global posture signal that attracts international talent and makes client-facing work seamless.
Comp
Role-and-level, not location
Covered in detail in the Compensation section — same role, same rate, regardless of geography. Two-tier pay for the same role destroys global culture faster than anything else.
Identity
The narrative signals the ambition
The website, case studies, and job descriptions should all signal global ambition from the first post. Aditya's external presence is the primary carrier of this — he sets the tone of who Verdera is in the world.
Storytelling
The Creative Lead embeds the global voice
The Creative & Storytelling Lead must write for a minister in Manila, a DFI analyst in Geneva, and a CFO in New York with equal fluency. Cross-cultural communication experience is not optional for this role — it is the job.
Remote model

No offices outside Bengaluru

Remote-first for all non-Bengaluru roles, with timezone availability as the sole constraint. No leases, no entities, no compliance overhead outside of what's needed. The quarterly all-hands in Bengaluru is the in-person culture anchor.

The Head of Deployment, Head of Customer Success, and Product Manager are the roles that hold this line.
